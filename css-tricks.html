Flex Wrap

Flex-Wrap: no-wrap

A Flex-Container with 8 items. The flex-direction is row, which is the default. And the flex-wrap is set to no-wrap, which is the default. Notice how the flex-items do not wrap at the end of the viewport, rather, a scroll bar is added and the go off the right side of the page. Notice that the flex-items that are outside of the visible area of the page are also outside of the containing flex-container.

justify-content: space-between

A Flex-Container with 4 items. The margin has been removed from the flex-items to show how the justify-content property works and the padding has been removed from the parent flex-container to show how space is distributed around the flex-items. When using the space-between property, the items have space distributed between them, but not around them. Notice how the first element is right up on the left-hand side of the container and the last element is right on the right-hand side of the container.

justify-content: space-around

A Flex-Container with 4 items. The margin has been removed from the flex-items to show how the justify-content property works and the padding has been removed from the parent flex-container to show how space is distributed around the flex-items. When using the space-around property, space is distributed evenly around the flex-items, with equal space being added to the right and left side of each item. This means that the items on the edge of the page will have half of the space as the space between the items in the interior of the page.

A Flex-Container with 4 items. The margin has been removed from the flex-items to show how the justify-content property works and the padding has been removed from the parent flex-container to show how space is distributed around the flex-items. When using the space-evenly property, we notice that the space around all items is equal, including the items on the left and right edge of the container.
