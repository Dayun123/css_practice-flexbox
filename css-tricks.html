Flex Wrap

Flex-Wrap: no-wrap

A Flex-Container with 8 items. The flex-direction is row, which is the default. And the flex-wrap is set to no-wrap, which is the default. Notice how the flex-items do not wrap at the end of the viewport, rather, a scroll bar is added and the go off the right side of the page. Notice that the flex-items that are outside of the visible area of the page are also outside of the containing flex-container.

Flex-Wrap: no-wrap, Flex-Direction: row-reverse

A Flex-Container with 8 items. The flex-direction is row-reverse, and the flex-wrap is set to no-wrap, which is the default. There should be 8 flex-items, but you will notice that the items that are outside of the viewport are pushed off the left side of the page, essentially making them invisible to the user.

Flex-Wrap: wrap, Flex-Direction: row-reverse

A Flex-Container with 8 items. The flex-direction is row-reverse, and the flex-wrap is set to wrap. If there are too many flex items to fit in the viewport on one line, they begin wrapping from the left-hand side (since the flex-direction is row-reverse) and start the next line below the current line on the right hand side.

Flex-Wrap: wrap-reverse

A Flex-Container with 8 items. The flex-direction is row, which is the default, and the flex-wrap is set to wrap-reverse. Flex-Wrap: wrap-reverse, flex-direction: row-reverse

A Flex-Container with 8 items. The flex-direction is row-reverse, so the items are laid out starting with the first item on the right hand side.
